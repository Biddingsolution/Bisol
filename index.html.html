ISO Certification

Get Certified. Win More Tenders.

ISO certification is often mandatory for government tenders above certain values. Our experts guide you through the entire process — from gap analysis to certificate issuance.

ISO 9001
Quality Management System
Most commonly required in government tenders. Demonstrates consistent quality standards.
ISO 14001
Environmental Management
Required for infrastructure, construction, and manufacturing tenders.
ISO 45001
Occupational Health & Safety
Mandatory for tenders involving site work, labor, or high-risk operations.
ISO 27001
Information Security
Essential for IT, software, and data-related government contracts.
Why ISO Matters for Tenders?

Many central government and PSU tenders require ISO certification as a mandatory eligibility criterion. Without it, your bid is disqualified before review. ISO certification also signals trust and quality to procurement officials, often giving you an edge in evaluation scoring.

Our ISO Certification Process
1
Initial Gap Analysis
We assess your current processes against ISO requirements and identify what needs to be implemented or improved.
2
System Design & Documentation
We build your Quality Management System (QMS) — all policies, procedures, forms, and work instructions tailored to your business.
3
Implementation & Training
We train your team to follow the QMS and ensure all processes are documented and running per ISO standards.
4
Internal Audit & Pre-Assessment
Mock audits and readiness checks so you walk into the certification audit fully prepared and confident.
5
Certification Audit & Certificate
We coordinate with the certifying body, provide audit support, and facilitate the issuance of your ISO certificate.
